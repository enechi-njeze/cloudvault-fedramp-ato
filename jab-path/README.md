## About the JAB

The **Joint Authorization Board (JAB)** is the primary governance body of the FedRAMP program. It is composed of representatives from three federal agencies:

| Agency | Representative (CloudVault FHX) |
|---|---|
| **Department of Defense (DoD)** | Col. Diana Marsh |
| **Department of Homeland Security (DHS)** | Frank Osei |
| **General Services Administration (GSA)** | Carol Whitfield |

These three agencies review the full authorization package — SSP, SAR, POA&M — and jointly decide whether to grant the Provisional ATO. Once a JAB P-ATO is issued, any federal agency can reuse that authorization without conducting their own full assessment.

## Why JAB P-ATO Matters for CloudVault FHX

CloudVault FHX serves 47 federal health agencies. Without a JAB P-ATO, each agency would need to independently review the authorization package and potentially conduct its own security assessment — a process that could take 6–18 months per agency and cost each one hundreds of thousands of dollars. The JAB P-ATO eliminates that friction entirely. One authorization, fully reusable across the entire federal government.

For a system at this scale, pursuing only an Agency ATO would be operationally and financially unsustainable.
